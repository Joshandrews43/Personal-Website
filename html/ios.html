Tender
A new way to order tenders to your door.
My brother and I have been talking about starting a business since we were 13, and Tender seems to be our first. My first (soon-to-be) published app, Tender brings the chicken tenders to you – if you live in Berkeley. Implementing MapKit, Braintree, Firebase, and a few other third party API's was a breeze, although we are currently looking for a designer so we can clean up our UI UX a little more.
Augmented Reality
Two cool ARKit apps
Using Apple's free augmented reality plugin, ARKit, I created a ruler that allows the user to tap on two points in 3D space and displays the 3D distance between them in meters. It was a relatively simple app to build, but I use it multiple times a week. Apple released a substantially more feature rich version in iOS 12, but I like using the version I made – food always tastes better when you cook it. The second app is an AR dice app that rolls 3D rendered die on any surface.

Humble Beginnings
Quick, easy, storyboarded apps to start out.
The first apps I made were following the course tutorial that my fraternity brothers bought me when they asked if I could learn Swift to make them an app. Although that project fell through, I stuck with the course, and began with these two simple, hardcoded apps. The one on the left is a Xylaphone that plays different tones per color, and on the right is a general knowledge quiz that introduced me to cocoapods. I had no idea how much I had yet to learn back then – these seemed like huge feats at the time.
